# Excel Fire Website Revamp Brief

Date: 23/04/2026  
Prepared for: Excel Fire Ltd (Matt Digby)

## TL;DR

Excel Fire needs a full website modernisation that keeps trust and compliance front-and-centre, improves visual quality, and converts commercial enquiries faster. The immediate build priority should be a clean, premium navbar and global design foundations (colours, type, spacing, buttons, cards, trust modules) so all following pages stay consistent.

  - `https://resinrock.com/`
  - `https://clmfireproofing.com/`

  - current hero/nav treatment
  - accreditation/clients section treatment

## Current Excel Fire Positioning (what already works)

- Strong core message: passive fire protection specialists, fire door expertise, reassurance-led language.
- Excellent trust assets already available: accreditations, client logos, testimonials, project credibility.
- Service breadth is clear (surveys, installations, remediation, compliance-focused delivery).

## Gaps to Solve in Revamp

- Visual identity feels dated (layout density, hierarchy, typography scale, CTA treatment).
- Navigation and information architecture need clearer user paths by audience and intent.
- Brand consistency is weak across section styling, spacing rhythm, and component behaviour.
- Conversion UX is not yet best-in-class (enquiry pathways, social proof placement, sticky actions).
- Excel Electrical offering is not structured as a clear sister proposition.

## What to Borrow from Reference Sites

## `cityfp.co.uk` (clarity + proof)

- Use bold headline plus immediate metrics (e.g., projects delivered, accreditations, operatives).
- Keep service explanation concise and outcome-focused.
- Prioritise clear “how we help” framing for specifiers, contractors, and FM teams.

## `allenglandgroup.co.uk` (service segmentation)

- Group services into distinct capability clusters for scanability.
- Make specialised service cards easy to browse from homepage.
- Include stronger “nationwide capacity + certified team” messaging.

## `gibbsonn.com/contact` (simple contact UX)

- Keep contact flow minimal and low-friction.
- Present one primary action path with clear phone and email fallback.

## `resinrock.com` (modern commercial polish)

- Strong CTA rhythm, section pacing, and modular card layout.
- Benefit-led messaging and ecosystem framing (homeowner/contractor/partner model can inspire audience split).
- Useful for visual tone and component consistency, not sector content.

## `clmfireproofing.com` (authority + lifecycle narrative)

- Excellent compliance-led positioning and full-lifecycle service story.
- Strong sectors/projects/news structure to support enterprise trust.
- Good model for integrating standards, legislation context, and digital QA narrative.

## Excel Electrical Page (new)

This should be intentionally simple, plain-English, and “Mrs Doris friendly”:

- Clear explanation of what Excel Electrical does.
- Domestic + small commercial services list in non-technical wording.
- Safety and certification reassurance.
- Simple quote request form and click-to-call actions.
- FAQs for typical homeowner/SME questions.

## Navbar First (build priority)

Desktop:

- Left: Excel Fire wordmark.
- Centre/right nav groups:
  - About
  - Services (mega menu)
  - Sectors
  - Projects
  - Accreditations
  - Articles/Insights (optional)
  - Contact (high-emphasis CTA style)
- Utility actions: phone, WhatsApp, and optional “Book assessment”.

Mobile:

- Sticky top bar with logo + menu trigger + call/WhatsApp quick action.
- Full-screen drawer with grouped links and one primary CTA.
- Keep tap targets large and hierarchy simple.

Behaviour:

- Sticky navbar after scroll.
- Subtle transparency/blur transition on scroll.
- Active link indicator and strong hover/focus states.

## Visual Direction

- Tone: premium, technical, trustworthy, modern.
- Typography: clean sans-serif pairing with stronger headline contrast.
- Palette:
  - Primary charcoal/near-black for authority
  - Secondary warm light neutrals for breathing space
  - Accent orange as conversion/action colour (retain brand recognition)
  - Structured semantic colours for success/warning/info
- Spacing: generous vertical rhythm; reduce clutter.
- Imagery: real project/site photography + compliant installation close-ups.
- Social proof: accreditations and client logos surfaced earlier in-page.

## Global Design System Starter (implement immediately after navbar)

- Tokens:
  - `colour` primitives (brand, neutral, semantic)
  - typography scale
  - spacing scale
  - radius, border, shadow, container widths
- Core components:
  - buttons (primary, secondary, ghost)
  - nav links + menu panels
  - cards (service, project, testimonial, accreditation)
  - trust strip (logos/badges)
  - section headers
  - form controls and validation states
- Interaction standards:
  - consistent hover/focus/active states
  - accessible contrast and keyboard navigation
  - reduced-motion friendly transitions

## AI Chat + WhatsApp Direction

- Add persistent WhatsApp CTA site-wide.
- Introduce AI assistant entry point for:
  - service guidance
  - quick qualification (project type, location, urgency)
  - contact handoff to team
- Keep legal/privacy notice clear for AI interactions.

## Phased Delivery Plan

1. Brand foundations: navbar + global tokens + shared UI primitives.
2. Homepage revamp: hero, trust strip, services snapshot, sectors, testimonials, CTA.
3. Service architecture: full services hub and subpages.
4. Excel Electrical page launch (simple, conversion-first).
5. Proof and authority modules: projects, accreditations, compliance content.
6. AI chat and WhatsApp enhancement.
7. Final polish: SEO metadata, performance, accessibility, QA.
